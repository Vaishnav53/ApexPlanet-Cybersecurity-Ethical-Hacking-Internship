# Reconnaissance

## Introduction

Reconnaissance is the first phase of ethical hacking and penetration testing. It involves gathering as much information as possible about a target before attempting any form of exploitation. The goal is to understand the target's infrastructure, technologies, network, and publicly available information.

Reconnaissance is also called Information Gathering or Footprinting.

---

# Objectives

- Identify target systems
- Discover IP addresses
- Find domain information
- Collect DNS records
- Detect running services
- Identify technologies
- Reduce uncertainty before scanning

---

# Types of Reconnaissance

## Passive Reconnaissance

Passive reconnaissance collects information without directly interacting with the target.

Examples:

- Google Dorking
- Whois Lookup
- DNS Records
- Shodan
- LinkedIn
- Public Documents
- Social Media

Advantages

- Difficult to detect
- No traffic sent to target
- Safe and legal for research

---

## Active Reconnaissance

Active reconnaissance directly interacts with the target.

Examples

- Ping
- Nslookup
- Banner Grabbing
- Traceroute
- Nmap
- Netcat

Advantages

- More accurate
- Real-time information

Disadvantages

- Can be detected
- May trigger firewalls or IDS

---

# Ethical Considerations

Reconnaissance should only be performed on systems you own or have explicit permission to test.
