# Cybersecurity Fundamentals

## Introduction

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, misuse, disruption, modification, or destruction. It combines technologies, processes, and best practices to safeguard digital assets against evolving cyber threats.

---

# CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is the foundational security model used to guide cybersecurity policies and controls.

## 1. Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized users.

### Examples

* Data encryption
* Access control mechanisms
* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

### Real-World Example

Online banking applications restrict access to account information to authenticated users only.

---

## 2. Integrity

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, consistent, and unaltered.

### Examples

* Hashing
* Digital Signatures
* File Integrity Monitoring

### Real-World Example

Software vendors provide SHA256 hashes so users can verify downloaded files.

---

## 3. Availability

Availability ensures that systems and data remain accessible when needed.

### Examples

* Load balancing
* Backup solutions
* Disaster recovery plans

### Real-World Example

Cloud service providers deploy redundant servers to minimize downtime.

---

# Common Cyber Threats

## Phishing

A social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information through fraudulent communications.

### Indicators

* Suspicious links
* Urgent language
* Spoofed domains

---

## Malware

Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access.

### Types

* Virus
* Worm
* Trojan
* Spyware
* Rootkit

---

## Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)

An attack that overwhelms a target system with excessive traffic, making services unavailable.

---

## SQL Injection

A vulnerability that allows attackers to manipulate database queries through unsanitized input.

---

## Brute Force Attack

An attack that attempts numerous password combinations until the correct credentials are discovered.

---

## Ransomware

Malware that encrypts victim data and demands payment for decryption.

---

# Attack Vectors

## Social Engineering

Manipulating human psychology to gain unauthorized access.

### Examples

* Phishing
* Vishing
* Pretexting

---

## Wireless Attacks

Attacks targeting wireless communication protocols and devices.

### Examples

* Evil Twin Attack
* Rogue Access Point
* WPA Cracking

---

## Insider Threats

Threats originating from authorized users who intentionally or unintentionally compromise security.

### Categories

* Malicious Insider
* Negligent Insider
* Compromised Insider

---

# Conclusion

Understanding the CIA Triad, common cyber threats, and attack vectors forms the foundation of modern cybersecurity practices and provides the basis for securing systems, applications, and networks.
